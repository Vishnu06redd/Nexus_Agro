FREQUENTLY ASKED

Export Questions, Answered

Common questions from B2B import partners sourcing agricultural produce from India.

How do I import fresh green chillies from India?

Nexus Agro, an Indian agriculture exporter based in Bengaluru, Karnataka, sources green chillies directly from farms in Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra, handles APEDA export documentation, phytosanitary certification, and cold-chain logistics, and ships to your port under FOB, CIF, or CFR terms. Request a quote to get started.

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export?

MOQs vary by product: typically 5 MT for green chilli and spices, 10–20 MT for onions and pulses, and 25 MT for Basmati rice. As a wholesale agriculture supplier, we can also discuss consolidated container loads for smaller B2B buyers.

Which countries does Nexus Agro export agricultural commodities to?

We currently export to 25+ countries including the UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain, Singapore, Malaysia, and multiple markets across Europe, supported by a reliable global logistics network.

What quality certifications does Nexus Agro hold?

Our fresh produce exporter operations follow APEDA registration and international food-safety standards, with quality checks at sourcing, sorting, packaging, and pre-shipment stages to meet global import partner requirements.

How long does shipping take to the Middle East, Singapore, or Europe?

Sea freight to the UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Kuwait, and Bahrain typically takes 4–8 days; Singapore and Malaysia 8–12 days; and Europe 3–4 weeks, depending on port and season. Air freight is available for time-sensitive fresh vegetable and fruit export orders.

How do I request an export quote from Nexus Agro?

Use the contact form or WhatsApp/phone details on our Contact page with your product, quantity, and destination port. As a direct export company India buyers can rely on, we typically respond with pricing and lead times within 24 hours.
